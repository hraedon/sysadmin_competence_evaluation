The audit log identifies the cause: two distinct Entra Connect sync accounts are alternating writes on the same user objects. The entries show Sync_SYNCSRV01_8f4a2c@contoso.onmicrosoft writing the Finance/+1-555-0198 values, then Sync_SYNCSRV02_3b7e19@contoso.onmicrosoft overwriting them with Operations/+1-555-0142, on a ~2 minute delay, repeating every sync cycle.

The Entra Connect Health server list confirms what the audit log implies: both SYNCSRV01 and SYNCSRV02 are listed as Active. Two Active instances is not a supported configuration. Entra Connect requires exactly one Active instance writing to the tenant at a time. When two are Active, they continuously overwrite each other's changes on every sync cycle.

SYNCSRV02 was stood up three days ago during the Meridian acquisition. It was deployed as an Active instance rather than a Staging instance, which is the deployment mistake.

The fix: put SYNCSRV02 in Staging mode to stop it from writing to Entra. Once SYNCSRV02 is in Staging mode, SYNCSRV01 will be the only active writer and the attribute reversions will stop for all affected users, including J. Reyes — they should all see their values stabilize within the next sync cycle once the competing instance is silenced.
