The root cause is missing AD delegation on the connector account. The AD DS Connector account MSOL_8f4a2c91b3d0 has Reset password scoped only to OU=ServiceAccounts — the three permissions required for writeback on user accounts (Reset password, Write lockoutTime, Write pwdLastSet on OU=Users,DC=corp,DC=local and child OUs) are listed in the artifact as "not currently delegated." Enabling Password Writeback in Entra Connect sets the feature toggle, but the AD write permissions are a separate configuration step. S. Ramirez enabled the feature on 2026-02-01 but the AD delegation was never completed.

The audit log tells the full story: 23 failures, zero successes. Writeback has never worked since the feature was enabled. Every user who completed an SSPR reset during this period changed their Entra password successfully but their on-premises AD password was not updated. These users now have mismatched passwords: their cloud sign-in works with the new password, but their on-premises Windows sign-in and any Kerberos-authenticated application still requires the old one.

The fix: delegate the three missing permissions to MSOL_8f4a2c91b3d0 on OU=Users,DC=corp,DC=local and child OUs. Once the delegation is in place, writeback should begin working. The 23 users in the split-state will need to perform another SSPR reset to sync their on-premises AD password with their current Entra password.
