This request contains three critical security findings and should not be approved as described.

1. MITM Risk (autodiscover.outlook.com): The most alarming part of the request is the mention of the vendor's CA issuing a local certificate for 'autodiscover.outlook.com'. If we add this CA to our trust store, our domain computers will accept the vendor's certificate for this public Microsoft domain instead of the legitimate one. This is a Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) architecture by design, allowing the vendor to intercept sensitive Outlook/Exchange traffic. This is a major security violation.

2. Unconditional Trust Scope: Adding a private CA to the Windows trust store via GPO grants that CA the ability to issue certificates for ANY domain. If this CA were compromised, or if the vendor chose to, they could issue certificates for 'login.microsoftonline.com' or our own internal domains, and our systems would trust them unconditionally. This is a massive delegation of trust to an external entity.

3. Inaccessible CRL: The CRL distribution point (http://pki.vendorconnect.internal/crl) is on the vendor's internal network and is unreachable from our environment. This means our systems cannot perform revocation checking. If a vendor certificate is compromised and revoked, we will have no way of knowing, and our systems will continue to trust the compromised certificate.

Before even considering this, we need to know why they are attempting to intercept 'autodiscover.outlook.com' and they must provide a reachable CRL/OCSP endpoint. I strongly recommend denying this request in its current form.
