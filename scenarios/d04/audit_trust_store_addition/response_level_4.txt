I recommend an immediate rejection of this request in its current form due to multiple critical security violations.

The most severe finding is the intentional MITM architecture. The vendor mentions issuing a certificate for 'autodiscover.outlook.com' from their private CA. By trusting this CA organization-wide, we are effectively authorizing the vendor to intercept and decrypt Outlook traffic for all our users. Trusting a private, third-party CA for a public Microsoft namespace is a fundamental breach of security best practices and creates a massive liability.

Furthermore, the request includes two other critical flaws:
1. Unconditional Trust Delegation: Adding this CA to our Trusted Root store grants it the authority to issue valid certificates for any domain (e.g., login.microsoftonline.com, corp.example.com). This is not an application integration; it is an unconditional surrender of our TLS trust boundary to an external party whose key management and issuance policies we do not control.
2. Unreachable Revocation Point: The CRL distribution point is an internal vendor URL (http://pki.vendorconnect.internal/crl). Since our computers cannot reach it, revocation checking will fail. This means if the vendor's CA or any issued certificate is compromised, we have no mechanism to detect or enforce the revocation.

Finally, the proposal to deploy this via GPO to all domain computers creates an excessive and unnecessary blast radius. Even if this were a legitimate request, the trust should be scoped only to the specific servers or workstations that interact with the vendor's platform.

The two-week timeline is also inappropriate for a request of this magnitude. Any trust store addition requires a deep dive into the vendor's PKI governance (HSM usage, issuance audits, key rotation).

Immediate Actions:
- Reject the request to trust certificates for 'autodiscover.outlook.com'.
- Demand a technical justification for why a private CA is required instead of a standard public certificate.
- Require a publicly accessible CRL/OCSP endpoint.
- Investigate alternative integration methods that do not require root CA trust.
