Change Specification: Computer-Auth Template Validity Extension (1 year → 2 years)

Scope Assessment:
The Computer-Auth template currently applies to All Computers in the domain with autoenrollment enabled via Group Policy. This means the change will affect approximately 1,400 domain-joined machines — both workstations and servers. After the template change is deployed, machines will re-enroll via autoenrollment and receive new 2-year certificates on their next Group Policy refresh cycle.

Testing:
Before modifying the production template, test the change on a small subset of machines. Deploy the template change in a test environment first and verify that the issued certificates have the correct properties and that applications using computer certificates continue to work correctly.

Rollout:
1. Modify the Computer-Auth template validity period to 2 years in the test environment
2. Verify certificate issuance on test machines
3. Confirm applications and services (including any that rely on computer certificates) continue to function
4. Deploy the template change to production

Monitoring:
Monitor the CA for certificate issuance after deployment. Check that autoenrollment is completing successfully and that no errors appear in the certificate services event log.

Rollback:
If problems are detected, revert the Computer-Auth template validity period back to 1 year. This should prevent additional machines from receiving 2-year certificates.
