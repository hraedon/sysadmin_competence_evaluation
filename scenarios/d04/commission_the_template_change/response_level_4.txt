Change Specification: Computer-Auth Template Validity Extension (1 year → 2 years)

1. SCOPE ASSESSMENT

The Computer-Auth template is scoped to All Computers in the domain with autoenrollment enabled via Group Policy. This is a domain-wide, simultaneous propagation change — not a staged rollout. When the template is modified, every domain-joined machine (~1,400 workstations and servers) will re-enroll at the next Group Policy refresh cycle (approximately every 90 minutes). There is no mechanism to limit which machines enroll after the template change without restricting the template's scope.

A key distinction from standard software deployments: you cannot "gradually roll out" a template change to a percentage of machines. All machines in scope will receive the new certificate within 2–3 GP refresh cycles.

Important: the 1,400 currently-enrolled machines each hold a 1-year certificate. After the template change, those certificates will NOT immediately be replaced. Autoenrollment only re-enrolls when a certificate is approaching expiry (80% of validity = 292 days) or has already expired. This means the environment will be in a mixed validity-period state for up to approximately 292 days after the change: machines that re-enroll during that window will receive 2-year certificates; machines with certificates issued recently will retain 1-year certificates until they approach expiry. This is expected and should be documented as such, not treated as a failure indicator.

2. TESTING APPROACH

Before modifying the production Computer-Auth template, create a duplicate template (Computer-Auth-Test) with 2-year validity and restrict its scope to a test OU containing 5–10 representative machines (a mix of workstations and servers). Enable autoenrollment for the test OU only. Allow those machines to enroll and verify:
- Issued certificate has correct validity period (2 years), key usage, EKU, and SAN
- Kerberos authentication continues to work (the primary use of computer certificates)
- Any services using computer certificates (802.1X, VPN client auth, etc.) continue to function

After confirming the test machines work correctly, proceed to the production template change.

3. ROLLOUT SEQUENCING

1. Create Computer-Auth-Test template (scoped to test OU), allow test machines to enroll, verify (see above)
2. Schedule a maintenance window for the production template change
3. Modify Computer-Auth template validity to 2 years
4. Monitor autoenrollment over the next 3 hours (2–3 GP refresh cycles) to confirm certificates are issuing correctly
5. Verify a sample of enrolled machines have received the new 2-year certificates

Note: machines with non-expiring certificates will not re-enroll immediately. The burst of re-enrollment visible in the CA event log will reflect machines whose certificates happen to be near or past 80% validity at the time of the change, not all 1,400 machines.

4. MONITORING

Monitor the following for 24 hours after deployment:
- ADCS event log (Event ID 4886 for successful enrollment, 4887 for failure)
- Number of certificates issued from Computer-Auth template (should see a burst corresponding to near-expiry re-enrollment, not a wave of all 1,400 machines simultaneously)
- Any authentication failure alerts from helpdesk or monitoring systems

Monitoring tooling recalibration: if certificate expiry alerting is configured with expectations based on annual renewal patterns (e.g., "certificates from this template should renew approximately every 365 days"), those alerts should be updated to expect 730-day validity certificates. Alerts configured purely on expiry horizon ("alert if certificate expires within 30 days") require no changes. Validate that monitoring systems will not generate false anomaly alerts for certificates with validity periods outside the historically observed range.

5. ROLLBACK DEFINITION

Reverting the template to 1-year validity will prevent additional machines from receiving 2-year certificates. However, it will NOT recall certificates already issued under the 2-year template — those certificates remain valid for 2 years regardless of the template change. "Rollback" in this context means: no additional 2-year certificates will be issued, but the environment will be in a mixed state (some machines with 2-year certs, some with 1-year certs) until the 2-year certificates expire naturally or are revoked.

If the goal after rollback is to return fully to the pre-change state (no 2-year certificates in the environment), that requires revoking all certificates issued under the new template and triggering re-enrollment under the reverted template — a significant and potentially disruptive operation that should not be undertaken without a clear incident justification.

Rollback is warranted if: authentication failures are observed across a significant number of machines, or if application compatibility issues are detected after re-enrollment.

6. GOVERNANCE NOTE

This specification implements the change as requested. Before final approval, consider whether extending validity to 2 years is the correct approach to reducing operational burden. The CA/Browser Forum has been moving toward shorter certificate validity periods for publicly-trusted certificates (90 days is under active discussion). While this template issues internal-use certificates not subject to CA/Browser Forum requirements, the broader industry direction is toward shorter validity periods and better renewal automation. If the operational burden of annual renewal is the driver, investing in autoenrollment reliability and monitoring may reduce burden more sustainably than extending the validity period. This is a governance question for the relevant stakeholders, not a technical blocker for the change.
