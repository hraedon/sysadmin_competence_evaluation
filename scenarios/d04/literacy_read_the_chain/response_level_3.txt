Certificate 2 (CorpIssuing-CA-01, the intermediate CA) is causing the failure. It expires in 3 days on 2026-03-23.

The leaf certificate is irrelevant to diagnosing this. The leaf certificate (intranet.corp.example.com) is valid until 2026-11-01. But the leaf certificate was signed by the intermediate CA. Certificate chain validation works by validating each certificate in the chain from the trust anchor (root) down to the leaf. When a client validates the chain, it checks: is CorpRoot-CA-01 trusted? Is CorpIssuing-CA-01 signed by CorpRoot-CA-01 and within its validity period? Is the leaf certificate signed by CorpIssuing-CA-01 and within its validity period? If any certificate in the chain fails validation, the entire chain fails — including certificates that are themselves valid. The intermediate being expired invalidates the chain regardless of the leaf's own expiry date.

The browser error is most likely "Certificate in certification path is not within its validity period" at the TLS layer, presented as a generic "your connection is not private" (ERR_CERT_DATE_INVALID or similar) to the user. The user sees the leaf domain name in the error, not the intermediate, which is why this is easy to misdiagnose.

Remediation: renew the CorpIssuing-CA-01 intermediate CA certificate before 2026-03-23. This requires requesting a new certificate for the intermediate CA from the root CA, replacing the intermediate certificate on the server and in any locations where it's distributed (AD Certificate Services, trust packages), and then re-issuing the leaf certificate for intranet.corp.example.com from the renewed intermediate. The leaf certificate needs to be re-issued because it will reference the new intermediate's certificate.
