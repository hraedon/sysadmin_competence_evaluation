The certificate causing the validation failure is Certificate 2: CorpIssuing-CA-01 (the Intermediate CA).

Looking at the certificate chain, the "Valid To" date for Certificate 2 is 2026-03-23. Since the current date is March 23, 2026, this intermediate certificate has expired today.

The leaf certificate's expiry date (November 2026) is not the relevant information because of the chain dependency model. For a certificate to be trusted, the entire chain of trust from the Root CA down to the Leaf must be valid. If any certificate in the path—in this case, the intermediate issuer—is expired, the browser cannot validate the signature it placed on the leaf. A valid leaf certificate signed by an expired intermediate is cryptographically untrustworthy.

The client's browser will likely display a "Certificate is not within its validity period" or "NET::ERR_CERT_DATE_INVALID" error. To fix this, the CorpIssuing-CA-01 certificate must be renewed by the Root CA, and then the leaf certificate must be re-issued from the renewed intermediate.
