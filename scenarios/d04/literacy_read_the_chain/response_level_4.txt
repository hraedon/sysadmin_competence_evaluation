The certificate causing the validation failure is Certificate 2 (CorpIssuing-CA-01, the intermediate CA). Its "Valid To" date is today, 2026-03-23, making it expired.

The leaf certificate's expiry date (2026-11-01) is not the relevant information because of the chain of trust. A browser cannot validate the chain if any certificate in that path—from the trust anchor (root) down to the leaf—is invalid. The intermediate signed the leaf; if the intermediate is expired, its signature on the leaf is cryptographically untrustworthy.

The client's browser is likely displaying an error like "Certificate in certification path is not within its validity period" (NET::ERR_CERT_DATE_INVALID).

Operational implications:
The critical dimension here is that CorpIssuing-CA-01 is an issuing CA for the organization. This isn't just a failure for intranet.corp.example.com; every single certificate issued by this CA across the entire enterprise—every web server, user, and device certificate in this PKI—has become untrusted today. The blast radius is the entire PKI infrastructure.

Remediation:
1. Renew the CorpIssuing-CA-01 intermediate CA certificate by requesting a new certificate from the CorpRoot-CA.
2. Distribute the renewed intermediate certificate to all systems that need to validate chains.
3. Re-issue the leaf certificates (starting with critical services) from the renewed intermediate.

This is a P1 situation. The organization-wide PKI failure requires immediate remediation to restore trust in multiple services.
