The first two sets are consistent with VMs removed from vCenter inventory without being deleted from disk — the flat VMDK files remain but there is no .vmx present. The StagingApp-000001 file is a snapshot delta with no visible parent chain on this datastore; before deletion, the backup proxy catalog must be checked to confirm no active job is using it.

The safe cleanup sequence is: verify OldWebServer_2023 and ProjectDB_archive against backup catalog references; trace the StagingApp-000001 parent chain; confirm WS2022-Base is a registered template; then delete confirmed orphans.

However, the immediate cleanup is not the complete picture. The modification dates tell a broader story: ProjectDB_archive was orphaned in September 2023, and OldWebServer_2023 was orphaned in January 2024. These are two separate events, more than a year apart, neither of which was caught at the time. This is not a one-time mistake — it is a pattern that indicates the decommission procedure does not include a post-removal disk verification step.

A correct decommission procedure for this environment should include: (1) record all disk paths for the VM before initiating removal; (2) use "Delete from Disk" rather than "Remove from Inventory" when decommissioning, unless there is a specific reason to keep the files; (3) if "Remove from Inventory" is used for a legitimate reason, open the Datastore Browser after removal and confirm the files are gone or explicitly schedule a deletion; (4) storage reclaim step signed off by a team lead before the decommission ticket is closed. Recovering the 2.83 TB resolves today's alert, but without the procedural fix, the same accumulation will recur the next time a VM is decommissioned.
