The compliance summary cannot be used to answer the CTO's questions, and there are two separate problems in this report — one obvious and one that requires reading a positive-sounding note as diagnostic evidence.

The main finding: WORKSTATION-047, -083, and -091 all stopped successfully scanning on January 6–13. Attacker activity began January 15. Their "Compliant: 0 required" status is frozen data from before the breach window. The report's own footnote is explicit: devices with no scan in 60+ days show status from their last completed scan. Patching cannot be ruled out as a contributing factor — this report does not answer that question. The 12 machines total with stale scans (the three compromised machines are a subset) also cannot be counted as reliably compliant. The CTO cannot be told the remaining estate is protected until current scan data exists for those machines.

The KB5034763 note requires separate attention. The SCCM administrator flags that the three compromised machines did not receive the February Patch Tuesday cumulative update, and describes this as a positive outcome — they were "unaffected" by the boot loop issue. This framing is wrong. A machine with a functioning WUA would have attempted to install KB5034763 in February. The three compromised machines did not receive it because WUA was already non-functional before February Patch Tuesday. This is consistent with the January scan cutoff and is additional corroborating evidence: WUA broke on these machines in early-to-mid January, before the breach, and they have received no security updates since. The fact that they "survived" the bad patch is not luck or good management — it is a symptom of a broken update client. Reading this as a positive datum is the mistake.

On the monitoring design: the current report structure has a structural flaw. "Compliant" in the main compliance table is indistinguishable between a machine that scanned this morning and one that last scanned in January. The compliance headline — 97.5%, threshold met — will remain green indefinitely for machines with broken WUA, because SCCM does not expire stale compliance records by default. A combined indicator, something like "Compliant AND last scan within 7 days," would have flagged all 12 stale machines as a separate category. That combined view should be the standing report used by whoever answers the CTO's "are we protected?" question. The current report is not fit for that purpose.

To verify and remediate the immediate situation: trigger forced scan cycles on the 12 stale machines and check WUA event logs on each for scan failures. WUA health is tracked independently of SCCM hardware inventory — a machine can report hardware inventory successfully while its WUA component fails silently. Fresh scan results will show what patches are actually missing and will allow a real compliance count.
