The CAB is not functioning as a risk management mechanism. There are two specific indicators that demonstrate this.

First, the near-zero rejection rate (0.4%) cannot be interpreted as evidence of high change quality without outcome data. An effective review process rejects or substantially modifies some percentage of what it reviews — not because the team writes bad plans, but because a genuine review identifies gaps that submitters miss. A rejection rate approaching zero is consistent with one of two explanations: (1) the team writes perfect plans every time, or (2) the review is not actually reviewing. Without outcome data, these cannot be distinguished. The CAB has 780 approved changes and no data on whether those approvals were correct.

Second, the CAB has conducted zero outcome reviews in 12 months. A risk assessment process that never compares its assessments to actual outcomes has no mechanism for improving. The change management system shows 47 changes required significant deviation from the approved plan (6.2% of executed changes) and 18 produced incidents. None of these were reviewed by the CAB. The most important data for calibrating the board's risk intuition — what actually happened to the changes it approved — is systematically absent.

These two structural gaps compound each other: without outcome data, the CAB cannot evaluate whether its risk assessments are accurate. Without accurate risk assessment, the CAB cannot improve. The 0.38% rejection rate cannot be interpreted as evidence of change quality until the CAB can compare its approvals to outcomes.
