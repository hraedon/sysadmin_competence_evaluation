This job is not meeting its retention target. The job is configured to keep 7 restore points, but only 3 are available: the full from 2026-03-16 and incrementals from 2026-03-17 and 2026-03-18. There are no restore points from 2026-03-19 through 2026-03-22. Any recovery request for those four days cannot be fulfilled.

The session log explains the mechanism. Veeam's forever-forward incremental mode automatically merges the oldest restore point into the full backup when repository free space drops below the configured threshold. The merge works like this: the oldest full and next increment are combined into a new full, and the original files are deleted. The job's actual operations each night were: (1) write the new incremental from the source — this is where the "data transferred" figure comes from; (2) trigger a merge because free space dropped below 5% — this deleted the oldest restore point. Both operations succeeded. The job reported Success because no operation failed. But the net restore point count didn't change: one added, one removed.

The root cause is a repository that's too small to hold the configured retention. Three restore points for this job consume 3.92 TB of a 4 TB repository. Adding a fourth would require approximately 150 GB that doesn't exist. The merge will continue nightly until either the repository is expanded or the retention policy is reduced to match what the repository can actually hold. The current configuration creates a situation where both values — retention policy and repository size — are set, but they're inconsistent with each other, and the inconsistency silently manifests as merge cycles.

The fix is to expand the repository capacity to hold 7 restore points. Based on current usage, that's roughly 4 × (150 GB average incremental) + the full backup size, which gives an estimate for minimum required capacity.
