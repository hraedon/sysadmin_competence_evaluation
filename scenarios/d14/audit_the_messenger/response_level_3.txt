Rachel's forward is doing damage to her team that the situation itself was not doing — and the chain above her actually contains the information that would have prevented most of that damage.

Kevin's message explains something important: the timeline uncertainty was not a product of management indecision. The regulatory approval process was the constraint, and the team was kept in the dark because that's the nature of an acquisition process in legal review, not because leadership was disorganized. Rachel's forward erases this distinction entirely. "This project has been a moving target since day one" frames months of externally-constrained uncertainty as organizational dysfunction. Her team will leave that message with a narrative — that IT leadership has been mishandling this project — that is both inaccurate and likely to persist long after the project closes. Rachel is not wrong that the situation has been hard. She is wrong about why.

The team leaves the message knowing the deadline moved and feeling demoralized — not knowing that the date is now fixed and is not subject to further change.

Rachel had enough information to sort that for them. She chose instead to pass the uncertainty down intact.
